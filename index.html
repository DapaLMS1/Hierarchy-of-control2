Eliminate ▼

Remove the hazard completely. This is the most effective control measure.

Substitute ▼

Replace the hazard with a safer alternative. For example, use a less toxic chemical.

Modify ▼

Change the way the work is done. This might involve redesigning a process or using different equipment.

Isolate ▼

Separate the hazard from people. Examples include using machine guards or enclosures.

Use Engineering Controls ▼

Incorporate technological solutions such as ventilation systems or noise dampeners.

Use Administrative Controls ▼

Implement safe work procedures, training, job rotation, and signage.

Use Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) ▼

Provide workers with equipment like gloves, masks, and eye protection. This is the least effective control and should be used as a last resort.
